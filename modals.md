# Modals

## Purpose

Use modals for focused, critical, or interruptive tasks without breaking the user's flow unnecessarily.

## Repeatable Practice

- Reserve modals for critical interactions, confirmations, progress, or focused subtasks.
- Avoid overusing modals for ordinary content.
- Provide clear escape routes: close button, cancel action, and appropriate outside-click behavior.
- Use explicit confirmation for destructive or irreversible actions.
- Avoid confusing negative wording in confirmation choices.
- Make modal content responsive and legible across devices.
- Use progress indicators for long operations.

## Development Checklist

- Focus is trapped inside the modal while open.
- The modal can be dismissed safely.
- Destructive actions are clearly labeled.
- Background content is inert while the modal is active.
- Mobile layout does not overflow the viewport.
