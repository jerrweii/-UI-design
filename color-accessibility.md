# Color and Accessibility

## Purpose

Use color as a functional guide while preserving readability and accessibility.

## Repeatable Practice

- Use color to show emphasis, status, affordance, brand, and hierarchy.
- Do not rely on color alone to communicate meaning.
- Check contrast for text, icons, buttons, and status indicators.
- Design in grayscale first when structure and hierarchy need validation.
- Use status colors consistently: success, warning, error, info, selected, disabled.
- Use intense colors sparingly for important actions or alerts.

## Development Checklist

- Text and controls meet expected accessibility contrast.
- Error states include text or icons, not only red styling.
- Brand colors do not reduce legibility.
- Interactive and non-interactive elements are visually distinguishable.
