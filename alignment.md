# Alignment

## Purpose

Use alignment to make screens easier to scan and process.

## Repeatable Practice

- Align text, controls, cards, and containers deliberately.
- Avoid mixing too many alignment axes in one section.
- Use left alignment for most dense reading and form layouts.
- Align repeated elements in lists and cards so users can compare quickly.
- Use grid and spacing rules to maintain alignment across screen sizes.

## Development Checklist

- Text baselines and component edges line up.
- Repeated cards or rows have stable dimensions.
- Icons and labels are visually centered.
- The eye can move through the layout without zig-zagging unnecessarily.
