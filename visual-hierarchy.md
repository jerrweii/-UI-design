# Visual Hierarchy

## Purpose

Make the most important content and action immediately clear.

## Repeatable Practice

- Use size, weight, contrast, spacing, and position to rank importance.
- Make one primary action visually dominant in focused views.
- Use secondary and tertiary styling for lower-priority actions.
- Avoid making every element visually loud.
- Use hierarchy to guide scanning before detailed reading.

## Development Checklist

- The primary action is visible without hunting.
- Headings, subheadings, body text, metadata, and actions are visually distinct.
- Important content is not hidden by decorative visuals.
- Disabled or secondary actions do not compete with primary actions.
