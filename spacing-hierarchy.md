# Spacing Hierarchy

## Purpose

Use spacing to show what belongs together and what should be read separately.

## Repeatable Practice

- Use small gaps for closely related elements.
- Use larger gaps between sections or unrelated groups.
- Avoid random spacing values.
- Make spacing symmetrical when possible.
- Use whitespace to improve readability and focus, not to fill empty areas.
- Treat whitespace as a functional hierarchy tool.

## Development Checklist

- Related labels, inputs, icons, and helper text are visually grouped.
- Section spacing is larger than component-internal spacing.
- Mobile spacing leaves enough breathing room without wasting space.
- There is no accidental crowding around key actions.
